YouTube playlists display videos in the order the creator set, with no built-in way to reorder them. If you want to watch the shortest videos first, find the most-liked content, or see the newest uploads — you are stuck scrolling manually.

## Why Sorting Matters

Being able to sort a playlist changes how you consume content:

- **Sort by duration (ascending):** Watch the shortest videos first when you have limited time. Perfect for squeezing in a few quick lessons during a lunch break.
- **Sort by duration (descending):** Identify the longest videos in a course so you can block out dedicated time for them.
- **Sort by likes:** Filter out low-quality filler and watch the content the community found most valuable.
- **Sort by views:** See which videos are most popular — useful for evaluating whether a course is worth your time.
- **Sort by upload date:** Watch the newest material first in channels that add content over time.

## Practical Examples

### Example 1: Quick Study Session
You have 20 minutes before a meeting. Sort the playlist by duration ascending — the shortest videos move to the top. Watch two or three quick lessons instead of starting a 45-minute lecture you cannot finish.

### Example 2: Evaluating a Course
Before committing to a 40-hour coding bootcamp playlist, sort by likes descending. If the first few videos have thousands of likes but the rest have barely any, the early content may be strong but the course might lose quality later.

### Example 3: Catching Up on a Series
A channel releases weekly tutorials. Sort by upload date descending to see the newest content first, then mark videos as watched as you go.

## Combining Sort with Video Range

You can also set a **video range** to analyze only a portion of the playlist. For example:

- Range 1–10 sorted by likes shows you the best introductory videos.
- Range 20–30 sorted by duration shows you which mid-course videos are longest (and may need extra focus).

This is especially useful for large playlists with 50+ videos where scrolling is impractical.

## Sort Is Not Available on YouTube Natively

YouTube's default view is creator-order only. The mobile app, desktop site, and TV app all lack sorting. This means:

- You cannot find the shortest video in a playlist without scanning the entire list.
- You cannot surface the most popular content within a curated collection.
- You cannot reorder by anything other than the upload order.
